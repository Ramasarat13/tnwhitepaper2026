The big picture

How fast did Tamil Nadu's debt grow?

In five years the State's debt nearly doubled — from ₹5.13 lakh crore to ₹10 lakh crore. As a share of the economy (GSDP), it has stayed stuck above the safe limit the whole time.

Debt stock & debt-to-GSDP, 2020-21 to 2025-26
Bars = total debt in ₹ Lakh Crore · Line = debt as a % of the State's economy (GSDP)
!The dashed red line is the 25% "danger zone" — the ceiling set by Tamil Nadu's own Fiscal Responsibility Act. The State has been above it every single year.

Understanding debt

Not all debt is equal — here's what's alarming

Borrowing can be smart or dangerous depending on what it's used for. Follow the coin across the three stages.

🪙
1
🏗️
Borrowing to BUILD
Building highways, hospitals, schools
This debt pays itself back through growth
2
💼
Borrowing to OPERATE
Paying salaries and daily expenses
Nothing is built. The bill still comes.
3
🌀
Borrowing to PAY INTEREST
Taking new loans to pay old loan interest
This is where Tamil Nadu is now
In 2025-26, Tamil Nadu paid ₹67,050 Crore in interest — more than it spent on roads, hospitals, and schools combined (₹50,911 Crore). The government is now borrowing just to survive, not to grow.
Living beyond means

Tamil Nadu spends more than it earns — every single year

The gap between what the government takes in and what it spends has only grown. That gap is plugged by fresh borrowing.

What the government earns vs what it spends (₹ Lakh Crore)
Green = total income · Orange = total spending
↳In 2025-26 the government spent ₹1.33 lakh crore more than it earned. This gap is filled by borrowing — which is exactly why the debt keeps climbing.

Think of it as a household: if the State were a family earning ₹10,000 a month, it would spend about ₹14,500 — and borrow the ₹4,500 shortfall — every single month.
